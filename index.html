About Africa

Africa is the world's second-largest continent by both area and population, known as the "mother continent" due to its history as the cradle of humankind. It has 54 countries, features diverse climates and terrains including deserts, rainforests, and savannas, and is surrounded by the Mediterranean Sea, Red Sea,Indian Ocean, and Atlantic Ocean. Key features include the Nile River, the Sahara Desert, and Mount Kilimanjaro.

Africa is an incredibly resource-rich continent, holding a large share of the world's natural resources, including significant mineral reserves like gold, diamonds, cobalt, and platinum, as well as oil, natural gas, arable land, and water. While these resources are vital for global industry and the continent's potential economic development, challenges remain in ensuring their sustainable use and ensuring local communities fully benefit from their extraction.
